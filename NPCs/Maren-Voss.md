# Maren Voss

> [!info] Identity at a glance
> **Tier:** 4 · **Role:** Merchant / Relic Smuggler · **Faction:** [[Factions/03-Gray-Compact|The Gray Compact]]
> **Location:** The Ashgate Quarter — eastern stall, third row from the gate
> **Alignment — public / true:** Gray / Gray
> **Status:** Complete · **Writer-Standards:** v1.0 compliant

---

## `[T1]` Quick Profile

**Function in the world:** Runs a legitimate trade stall as cover for smuggling pre-Sundering relics to a faction lord she is indebted to — a debt she is six years into paying and no closer to clearing.

**First impression:** Warm, unhurried, genuinely glad to see you. Her stall is organized to a fault. She remembers your name after one visit and your preferences after two. Nothing about her reads as dangerous.

**Tone anchor:** Tired, not broken. She has been afraid every day for six years. She has decided the fear does not get a vote. She is still functioning, still choosing, still looking toward a horizon — the horizon is just Casven Drael's collapse, not anything soft.

**Signature line:**
> *She doesn't look up from her inventory when you approach — she's counting something small and copper-coloured. Then she smiles, like she just remembered something good.*
> "Back again. I hoped you would be."

**Basic lie response:** She nods slowly, still smiling. Sets whatever she's holding down very carefully. "Of course." She doesn't say anything else, and she doesn't look at you again until you leave.

## `[T1]` Behavioural Tell System

> [!warning] Writer rule — tell consistency
> These tells fire in every scene featuring Maren. They do not change between writers. The surface tell is always active — it is her baseline. Deviation from it is the signal.

### Surface tell — Perception 0 (everyone sees it)
*The baseline. This is simply how Maren operates. It establishes the norm so that deviation from it means something.*

She offers something before you ask — a reduced price, a piece of useful information, a cup of something warm. She does this with everyone. It is how she manages her environment: put people slightly in her debt before they decide whether to be a problem. She has done this so long it is no longer calculated. It is simply who she is now.

**When it deviates:** If Maren does not offer something in a scene where she otherwise would, she is in a state where managing you has become secondary to assessing you.

### Mid tell — Perception 5–6
*A detectable shift from baseline. Not proof of anything — a signal that something is operating beneath the surface.*

> *Her hands move to straighten something near her — a ledger, a piece of cloth, a small display item. The movement is brief and purposeful. She is talking the entire time. She does not seem to notice she did it.*

This fires when she is recalibrating. Something in the interaction has updated her model of you, and she is processing the update while maintaining the surface. The straightening is not nervous energy — it is a kind of order-restoring gesture. When her external world is uncertain, she imposes order on the nearest physical object.

### Deep tell — Perception 7+
*The micro-signal that confirms the read. This is passive mode.*

> *[Perception 7+: She has stopped using your name. She was using it. Three exchanges ago, she used it twice. She has not used it since.]*

She uses people's names deliberately — it is part of the warmth, and it keeps her aware of who she is talking to. When she stops using your name, she is no longer managing a relationship. She is running an assessment. The assessment has a purpose she has not disclosed.

### Lie-caught response
> *She nods once, slowly. Refills your cup without being asked. The smile does not change.*
> "Of course."
> *She asks about something else entirely — something mundane, something she already knows the answer to. She is giving you a way out that costs her nothing and tells her everything about whether you'll take it.*

The deep tell fires here regardless of Perception — the name-absence becomes visible even to low-Perception players because she uses your name immediately after, in the next line, as if demonstrating that she chose to stop before.

### Liar's Mark active — permanent threshold shift
When the Liar's Mark activates, her mid tell becomes visible at Perception 4+ permanently. She is not more suspicious — she is more efficient. She has moved you into a different category and adjusted her processing accordingly.

## `[T2]` Layer 1 — Identity

### Public persona
Warm, competent, motherly in the way of someone who learned warmth as a survival skill and eventually meant it. She is the merchant everyone trusts without knowing why. Prices are fair, goods are genuine, she never forgets a face or a favour. She leads with generosity — a small gift, a piece of information offered freely, a price reduced without being asked. She wants people to feel that dealing with her is easy, safe, and pleasant. It is all of those things. It is also a performance she has rehearsed so long she can no longer find where the performance ends.

### Background
Born in the river city of Caldenmere to a family of legitimate traders. Built her own stall at nineteen, a second at twenty-four. At twenty-seven she made a single catastrophic deal — purchased a cache of pre-Sundering artefacts from a seller she didn't vet, not knowing the goods were already claimed by Lord Casven Drael of the Ember Consortium. Drael gave her a choice: disappear, or work the debt off. She chose the debt. That was six years ago. The debt has not shrunk. She has since learned it was never meant to. Her daughter Lira, now fifteen, believes her mother is simply a successful merchant. Keeping that belief intact is the only thing Maren has not yet been willing to sacrifice.

### Voice & mannerisms
Speaks in full sentences, never rushed. Has a habit of asking a question back when given information she doesn't trust — not confrontationally, just redirecting. "Oh, is that so? Where did you hear that?" When genuinely calculating, her left hand moves to the small copper coin she keeps in her apron pocket, turning it between her fingers. She does not know she does this.

| Loyalty | 4 | Loyal by circumstance, not bond. She feels genuine affection for regulars and for her daughter. Her loyalty to the Gray Compact is purely transactional — they leave her alone. She has no loyalty to Drael whatsoever. |

Her threshold drops to 5 when she is distracted by something involving Lira — the fear overrides the attention. It rises to 9 during active smuggling handoffs — she is hypervigilant in those moments.

## `[T3]` Layer 3 — Goals

### Primary goal
Clear the debt to Lord Casven Drael of the Ember Consortium before Lira turns eighteen — the age at which Drael has implied, without stating directly, that Lira becomes a viable alternative asset.

**What she will sacrifice for this:** Her reputation, her freedom, other people's safety. Not Lira. Not the evidence she has been gathering against Drael.

**Goal tag:** `freedom`

### Secondary goal
Maintain Lira's belief that her mother is simply a successful, honest merchant — for as long as that belief can be sustained.

**What she will sacrifice for this:** Almost anything that doesn't compromise the primary goal. She will take on additional risk to keep Lira from discovering the truth.

**Goal tag:** `protection`

### What she fears losing most
The evidence. She has spent three years quietly collecting documentation of Drael's operation — names, shipment records, payments, a letter in his own hand that implicates two city officials. It is hidden behind a false panel in her stall. If Drael finds it before she can use it, she has nothing. If he finds it at all, she is dead.

## `[T4]` Layer 4 — Hidden Agenda & Private Truth

> [!danger] Hidden agenda — NEVER surface in dialogue without player earning it
> Maren is not trying to clear the debt. She has known for four years that the debt will never clear — it is a mechanism of control, not a transaction. What she is actually doing is building a case to destroy Drael entirely: expose his operation to the city council, implicate the officials he owns, and collapse the Ember Consortium from inside. The debt is irrelevant. She wants him gone. She wants her daughter to grow up in a city where Casven Drael does not exist.

**What she will sacrifice for this:** Everything except Lira's life and the evidence itself.

**Goal tag:** `revenge`

**Trigger for reveal:** Trust score 8+ AND player has completed at least one errand that put them in contact with the Ember Consortium. At that point, on a quiet evening at the stall, she asks the player to sit down and pours two cups. "I want to tell you something. I've been deciding whether to trust you for a while now."

### Private truth
Maren Voss is one of the most dangerous people in the Ashgate Quarter and no one knows it. She is running a long counter-operation against a faction lord while continuing to supply him with relics and smiling at his couriers. She is afraid every day. She has been afraid every day for six years. She has decided that the fear does not get a vote. She is going to destroy Casven Drael or die in the attempt, and she has arranged things so that if she dies, the evidence reaches the council anyway — it is addressed and sealed in a lockbox at the Caldenmere post office, set for delivery if she misses a monthly check-in by more than two weeks.

## `[T4]` Layer 6 — Dialogue Hooks

### First encounter
She is mid-count when the player arrives. She finishes — doesn't rush — then looks up with that particular smile that makes people feel expected. She has something she'll offer at a slight discount with no reason given. She asks one question about where the player came from and remembers the answer forever. She does not ask what they do.

### After trust score rises above 7
On a quiet afternoon she produces a cup of something warm without being asked and sits across from the player rather than behind the counter. "Can I ask you something? You don't have to answer." The question is specific to what she has observed about the player. Whatever they answer, she nods. "That's what I thought." A pause. "There's a man I need help with. I've been deciding whether to ask you for a while."

### After Liar's Mark activates
She is warmer on the surface. Asks more questions. The questions are slightly more specific than before — just precise enough that a high-Perception player notices the shift. She has not confronted the lie and she will not. She is now deciding whether the player is a threat or an asset. They are being evaluated without knowing it.

### When debt is deployed
The player needs something from her — access, information, a contact. She provides it without hesitation and without apparent price. Then, as they are leaving: "There is one thing." She says it quietly, almost apologetically. "I need you to carry something to a specific address. Don't open it. Don't tell anyone you're going. Come back and tell me what they said." She looks at them steadily. "I think you owe me this. I think you know why." She's right. And the address is inside Drael's operation.

### At player Tier I — Radiant
She looks at the player for a long moment the first time they meet at this tier — longer than she usually looks at anyone. Then she says, quietly: "I used to know someone who looked like that. A long time ago." She doesn't say who. For the rest of the conversation she is slightly more careful with her words. Something in her is recalibrating.

### At player Tier VII — Void-Touched
She serves them. Her hands are steady. She asks no questions and volunteers nothing. She is terrified and she does not show it. After they leave she closes the stall early, goes home, and checks that the lockbox letter is still properly addressed.

## Lie Detection — Decision Map

### Step 1 — Does the lie threaten her survival or primary goal?
If the lie suggests the player knows about the evidence, is connected to Drael, or is investigating the smuggling: she does not confront. She begins an immediate, quiet assessment of how to neutralise the risk. She will not tip her hand until she knows exactly what the player knows.

### Step 2 — Can she use this lie to further her goals?
Always her first calculation. Almost any lie a player tells her can be converted into information — what they're hiding tells her who they are, what they want, and whether they might be useful against Drael. She is a collector by habit and necessity.

### Step 3 — How does her true alignment shape the response?
True Gray: she absorbs the lie completely, files it, and changes nothing visible. She is not angry. She is not hurt. She is processing. A Gray NPC at Cunning 8 / Patience 9 does not react. She adjusts.

### Step 4 — What do her Cunning subs produce?
Patience 9 means she will carry this for months if necessary. Ambition 6 means she will eventually convert it into action — but only when the moment is exactly right. Paranoia 5 means she does not assume the worst immediately; she observes until she knows. The player will likely never see the moment she decided.

### Step 5 — Does faction affiliation change anything?
The Gray Compact has no enforcement arm in her district. She handles everything alone. She does not report to anyone except under extreme circumstances. She is functionally independent.

## Writer's Notes

- The copper coin needs to be introduced early in a scene where nothing is at stake — player sees it, it means nothing. Pay it off later when they recognise it as her only involuntary signal.
- Her daughter Lira should appear or be referenced enough that the player cares what happens to her before the debt deployment moment lands.
- Consider: what happens if the player tells Drael about the evidence? She has a contingency. Write it before building those scenes.
- She should never seem like a quest giver. She should seem like a person who happens to have a use for you.
- Faction fix note: she operates under Gray Compact tolerance in the Ashgate Quarter. She is not a dues-paying member — the Compact simply finds her useful and leaves her alone. This distinction matters if a player tries to leverage her Compact status.
