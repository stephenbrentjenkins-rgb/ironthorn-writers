# The Greenward

## Overview
Parks, healing temples, and the only growing things in Ironthorn that look genuinely healthy. The Greenward occupies the western edge of the upper city and has been there, in some form, longer than the rest of the city has existed. The trees in the central grove predate the city's founding by at least a century. The Verdant Circle did not plant them. The Circle arrived and found them, which is why the Circle stays.

The district is quieter than it should be given its central location. Sound behaves differently under the old canopy. Nobody has ever adequately explained this.

## Atmosphere
Elwynn Forest reference — cathedral light through old growth, a sense that something has been here longer than the human structures around it and will remain after they are gone. The Greenward is not threatening. It is patient.

## Locations

### Inn — The Canopy Rest
A modest inn built against the eastern wall of the Greenward, where the district transitions toward the Sanctum Ward. Quieter than the Hearthstone — fewer guests, smaller common room, better food. The cook sources everything from the district's herbalist and the city's outer farms. The innkeeper, **Perris** (Tier 1), is a retired ranger with Circle connections he does not advertise. Players with Circle standing notice small things: a pressed leaf on the room's windowsill, a specific tea offered without being asked for.

*Preferred rest location for Circle-affiliated players. Storage access available. Quieter than the Hearthstone — fewer eavesdroppers.*

---

### General vendor — Tallow & Seed
A small general store near the district's main path entrance, selling food, candles, rope, basic tools, and seeds. Run by an older couple (both Tier 1) who have operated in the Greenward for thirty years. They are unfailingly pleasant and carry an unusually complete stock of natural-origin materials alongside the standard inventory. The seeds they sell include two varieties unavailable anywhere else in the upper city — Circle-sourced, sold without comment.

*Alignment-neutral pricing — the Greenward's vendors do not price by faction.*

---

### Repair station — Renn's Workbench
A small repair operation in a converted garden outbuilding. **Renn** (Tier 1) handles equipment maintenance, tool sharpening, and light leatherwork. Not a forge — does not work metal beyond basic repairs. What he does do well: natural-material gear. Leather armour, bone components, wood-and-sinew items. Circle-sourced materials repaired here retain their properties; the same materials repaired elsewhere sometimes do not. Renn knows this and does not explain why.

*Specialises in natural-material equipment. For metal work, players are directed to the Ashgate forge.*

---

### Guard post — Greenward Watch
The smallest guard post in Ironthorn — two officers, rotating shifts, a small stone building near the district's southern entrance. The Greenward Watch is a city appointment, not Covenant-directed, and the officers here reflect that: less formal, less watchful, more focused on keeping the parks clear of trouble than on monitoring who comes and goes. The current officer, **Warden Duss** (Tier 1), knows Grove-Keeper Sennath personally and gives the Circle's station a wide berth without being asked.

*Lowest surveillance density of any upper-city district. Alignment flags do not trigger here below Tier VI.*

---

### Notice board — The Grove Board
A wooden board mounted near the district's central fountain — handwritten notices, community announcements, lost-animal postings, and seasonal event notices. No Covenant oversight. The Grove Board is where the Greenward's residents communicate with each other, and where the Circle occasionally posts veiled notices that only Circle-affiliated readers understand. Players with Circle rank 2+ can read the second layer of meaning in certain postings.

---

### General trade broker — Sellen's Exchange
A small brokerage operating from a converted greenhouse at the district's eastern edge. **Sellen** (Tier 2) specialises in natural materials, botanical reagents, and goods sourced from outside the city — specifically, goods that arrive through Circle-adjacent trade networks. Her inventory rotates with the seasons. She will not deal in goods she considers ecologically harmful, and she will tell you so directly if you try.

*Greenward's trade broker has the narrowest inventory of any district but the most consistent access to rare natural materials.*

---

### Alchemist — Verath's Still
Run by **Verath of the Still Hand** (Tier 3) — also the head cleric of the Temple of the Mending Hand, which creates a slight jurisdictional oddity the city has decided not to examine. The Still operates from a back room of the temple and produces healing compounds, restorative tonics, and natural-reagent formulations that Mirren in the Sanctum Ward technically cannot make because they require unlicensed botanicals. The Circle's reagent garden is the source. Everything produced here is legal by a reading of city law that Verath could defend in the Office of the Record if pressed. She has not been pressed.

*Access to Circle-reagent formulations unavailable in the upper city's licensed alchemy shops. Circle standing unlocks additional inventory tiers.*

---

### District-exclusive — Herbalist and reagent garden
The Circle's grove-keeper manages a cultivated garden adjacent to the Grove of First Standing. This is the only location in Ironthorn where Circle-sourced botanical reagents are available for purchase or trade.

**Grove-Keeper Sennath** (Tier 3) manages both the grove and the garden. What is available depends on Circle standing:

| Standing | Access |
|---------|--------|
| None | Basic herbs; standard healing plants |
| Rank 1 | Reagents for standard alchemical use |
| Rank 2 | Rare botanicals; Circle-ritual components |
| Rank 3 | Grove-memory reagents — materials that carry impressions of what the grove has witnessed |
| Rank 4+ | Root Court-sourced materials; story-locked until Root Court arc progresses |

*Some reagents only become available after specific story events. The garden knows things the grove-keeper does not always volunteer.*

---

### City management office — Office of the Commons
Handles parks maintenance, public water access, open land disputes, and construction permits affecting green space. Nominally independent — the only city office with no direct faction supervisor. In practice, the head clerk is quietly Circle-affiliated and has quietly denied seventeen construction permits in twelve years on grounds that the relevant site contains "ecologically significant root structures."

**Head officer:** Commons Clerk **Iravel** (Tier 2) — careful, precise, and in possession of a survey map that shows the Greenward's root system extending significantly further beneath the city than any official document acknowledges. She has not filed this map with the Office of Works. She keeps it in her desk.

**Quest function:** Land disputes, construction obstruction quests, and the beginning of the Root Court storyline's above-ground thread all pass through this office. Iravel's survey map is a mid-game document unlock.

## Alignment interaction
- All alignment tiers may enter and receive healing at the Temple of the Mending Hand
- The Grove of First Standing responds to alignment: Tier I receives clearest impressions; Tier VII receives silence — which is itself information
- The Circle's coherence check applies: performing light alignment while carrying dark-alignment flags causes Sennath and Verath to become cooler on subsequent visits — not hostile, just less forthcoming
- Alignment flags below Tier VI do not trigger the Greenward Watch

## Connections
- East: Sanctum Ward (open)
- South: Ledger Row (open)
- Below: The Hollows — beneath the oldest tree (Circle rank 5+ or specific quest)
