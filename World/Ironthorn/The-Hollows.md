# The Hollows

## Overview
Ironthorn's original sewer and aqueduct system — built three hundred years ago, expanded twice, abandoned during an event that is not in any official record. The city's current drainage infrastructure was built above it. Nobody went back down to remove the original layer because nobody wanted to go back down.

The Hollows connect every district beneath street level. This includes connections between districts separated above ground by the light/shadow divide. The divide does not exist below the surface. Whatever it is and wherever it originates, it does not extend underground. This is among the most significant things the Hollows contain. It is not the most significant.

## Design reference
EQ Qeynos Aqueducts / Bloodsaber tunnels — infrastructure repurposed, sub-city with its own continuity, older than what is above. WoW Raven Hill undercurrent — the sense that what is underground has been there longer and has not stopped.

## Locations within the Hollows

### Rest point — The Dry Chamber
The only safe rest location in the Hollows. A large, dry, structurally sound chamber approximately at the geographic centre of the sub-city — beneath Ledger Row, where the surface divide should apply and does not. The chamber was clearly used as a regular meeting point at some point in the past: stone benches, iron rings set into the walls for lanterns, a fire pit with evidence of use that is not recent enough to explain. Players who rest here do not trigger any surface-faction surveillance. No NPC can follow a player into the Hollows without the same access requirements the player had to meet.

*The only fully neutral rest point in Ironthorn. No faction surveillance reaches here. Safe storage access.*

---

### No general vendor
There are no vendors in the Hollows. What the Hollows contain is information, access, and the things that were left behind when people stopped coming. Players must supply themselves before entering.

---

### No standard repair station
Equipment cannot be repaired in the Hollows. This is a deliberate pressure mechanic — extended time below ground degrades the player's operational capability and creates a reason to surface.

---

### No guard post
The Office of Works has jurisdiction on paper. No city officer has been below street level since the sealing. The gap this creates is where three separate questlines begin.

---

### Notice — The Communication Markers
Throughout the Hollows, certain corridor walls carry markings in a notation system that does not match any known faction's code. The markings indicate routing — which passages are maintained, which have been allowed to deteriorate, and which are marked with a symbol that appears nowhere in any surface document. Players with Perception rank 7+ begin noticing the markers. Players with Perception rank 9+ notice that the markers are not all old — some are recent. Something is still maintaining the Hollows and leaving directions for itself.

*Not a notice board. A navigation system for something that still uses the Hollows regularly.*

---

### No standard alchemist
However: certain chambers contain residue from alchemical work that is not recoverable but is identifiable. Players with alchemical knowledge can identify what was being produced in specific chambers. This is part of the Ashen Veil's questline.

---

### No city management office
See above. The Office of Works has jurisdiction. It has declined to exercise it. This is the most consequential act of administrative avoidance in Ironthorn's history.

## What is down there — by discovery layer

### First layer — infrastructure
The original aqueduct channels, now dry. Stone corridors, vaulted ceilings, construction markings still visible. Old. Structurally sound. Empty in the sense that nothing has lived here recently — or nothing that leaves traces a surface person recognises as traces.

### Second layer — occupation evidence
Evidence of use since the sealing. Storage caches of varying ages. Communication markers. Certain corridors maintained while others deteriorate. Ongoing maintenance by something with preferences about routing.

### Third layer — the original event
A dry chamber beneath the Sanctum Ward — connected to the Hall of Records sealed sub-basement — contains the city's founding documents and the records of what actually happened to cause the light/shadow divide. Players who access this chamber receive the primary lore revelation of the Ironthorn arc. The Covenant has known this chamber exists. They sealed the sub-basement access from their side three generations ago. The records have been down here, unread by any living person, for sixty years.

### Fourth layer — what stopped the cartographer
The eastern section, beneath the Wound Market. The Compact cartographer's final note: *"further passage blocked — not by structure — recommend discontinuation."* What is in this section does not block passage physically. Players can walk through it. What stops them is something they cannot name — a sense that the space is aware, that being here is being observed, that whatever is observing has been doing so for a very long time and has not yet decided what to do about the observation.

Players who push through reach the Void Eternum's Ironthorn anchor. At Perception rank 10, the player sees what the cartographer saw: the observation is not passive. It is cataloguing.

## The missing Compact agent
Last seen entering through the Ledger Row sixth-office passage four months ago. Notes recovered from their room at the Neutral Table end mid-sentence: *"the eastern section is not empty in the way that the rest of it is empty. Something is —"*

Factor Saul has not assigned a replacement. The dossier is still open.

At Perception rank 10, in the eastern section, the player finds a single additional page that was not recovered: a hand-drawn diagram of the observation pattern the agent was mapping. The diagram is almost complete. The last mark was made at the point where the agent's notes end.
