# The Wound Market

## Overview
Named for what it sells. Pain-bonding contracts, bound-blood services, rare materials with no respectable origin. The Crimson Throne does not operate the Wound Market officially. It operates the people who operate it. The market runs in daylight. This is the most unsettling thing about it. It is not hidden. It is somewhere that most people choose not to go, and the city has made its peace with that choice.

## Atmosphere
Not dramatic evil, but normalised evil. The vendors are not theatrical. The customers are not ashamed. A food vendor near the entrance sells hot flatbread. Children run between stalls on errands. An old man argues with a vendor about pricing. These things coexist with what is actually being sold, and no one in the market finds the coexistence remarkable.

Reference: Darkshire, WoW — tired pragmatism about living near something terrible. Neriak, EQ — commerce conducted in darkness that considers itself the natural order.

## Locations

### Inn — The Restless House
A lodging house at the market's northern edge that faces Ledger Row — the side of the building that looks toward the upper city is kept cleaner than the side that faces the market. The proprietor, **Sel Harrow** (Tier 2), is a former Offered who bought out his contract twelve years ago and has since built a modest operation catering to Wound Market regulars, Throne-affiliated visitors, and the occasional upper-city curiosity-seeker. He knows exactly who every guest is and what they are doing in the Market. He does not volunteer this information. He does sell it, selectively, to The Collector.

*Preferred rest for Throne-affiliated and dark-alignment players. No alignment check on entry. Sel's information network makes him a passive intelligence source for The Collector — and for players who earn his trust.*

---

### General vendor — The Open Stall
Not one vendor but three, rotating weekly, operating from the market's entrance row. General goods — food, tools, consumables — with a lower-city price and a lower-city attitude toward provenance. The goods here are functional. Some of the packaging has been replaced. The vendors do not draw attention to this.

*Standard consumables at lower-city prices. Some inventory of clearly non-standard origin. Alignment-pricing strongly inverted: Tier VI–VII receive premium goods at reduced prices. Tier I–II are served the same goods at higher prices and with a quality of attention that makes them want to leave.*

---

### Repair station — The Mender
An unmarked repair operation two stalls into the market. The operator, known only as **the Mender** (Tier 2), is the only person in Ironthorn who will repair blood-magic-enhanced equipment without either Throne standing or an extended explanation. The work is excellent. The Mender's personal alignment is not established — they are one of the few NPCs in Ironthorn whose true alignment even high-Perception players cannot read. This is not accidental.

*Will repair anything. Specialises in blood-magic-enhanced and Throne-sourced equipment. True alignment unreadable — even at Perception rank 10.*

---

### Guard post — Market Oversight
Three officers assigned to the Wound Market. Their function is officially described in city records as "commercial dispute resolution and public safety." In practice, they stand near the market entrance, do not enter the market proper, and have not filed a single incident report in four years. They are paid by the Treasury in Ledger Row. Their actual instructions come from The Collector, who conveys them verbally, and from the Office of Tolerance, whose head officer they have a weekly meeting with.

**Oversight Lead Penra** (Tier 2) is not a corrupt officer in the sense of having been bought. She is a pragmatic officer who has concluded that the Wound Market is stable and that stability is her mandate. She is correct that the Market is stable. She has not examined what maintains the stability.

*Present at entrance only. Do not enter the market. File no reports. The most institutionally compromised guard post in Ironthorn.*

---

### Notice board — The Market Board
No board. Information in the Wound Market travels through the Quiet Room, through The Collector's Overseer's Seat, and through the personal networks of the vendors. Posting a notice in the Wound Market means handing it to the right person, not pinning it to wood. Players learn this quickly. Upper-city players learn it more slowly and at greater cost.

*No physical board. Information economy is entirely personal and relationship-gated. First introduction to the lower city's oral information network.*

---

### General trade broker — The Materials Row
The market's back section functions collectively as the trade brokerage — no single broker, but a set of vendors dealing in materials with unclear provenance. Components for alchemy, necromancy, and blood magic not available through legitimate vendors. Some sourced from the Ashen Veil's Ashgate operations. The vendors do not ask where things came from and do not say where they source their own inventory.

*Dark-economy materials not available anywhere else in the city. No origin questions. No consignment service — cash transactions only.*

---

### Alchemist — The Bound Still
Operated by a Throne-affiliated alchemist known as **Kess** (Tier 2) from a permanent stall in the market's western section. Produces compounds that require pain-sourced or blood-derived reagents as base components — a category that Mirren, Verath, and Pell alike do not work in, for different reasons. The Bound Still's formulations are effective, durable, and carry an alignment cost per use that is noted in the item description and ignored by the people who buy them.

*Blood-reagent alchemy unavailable anywhere else in Ironthorn. Each use of a Bound Still compound costs −1 alignment. Throne standing unlocks additional formulation tiers.*

---

### District-exclusive — The Blood-Bound Arcanist
**Arcanist Vorell** (Tier 3) operates from a permanent structure at the market's centre — the only building in the market that has a door rather than a stall front. Enchantments, binding rituals, and blood-magic services beyond what the standard bound-blood practitioners offer.

**Access:** Throne standing rank 2+ or alignment Tier V or lower.

**Services by standing:**

| Access | Available services |
|--------|-------------------|
| Tier V or Throne rank 1 | Basic binding rituals; minor pain-transfer enchantments |
| Throne rank 2 | Weapon enchantments; armour blood-binding; enhanced pain contracts |
| Throne rank 3 | Permanent enchantments with alignment cost per use |
| Throne rank 4+ | Dual-binding — enchantments that affect both the item and an NPC of the player's choosing |

**Vorell's records:** Every commission is logged. Name, item, enchantment, date. The log is sent to Pain-Priest Korrath weekly. This is not disclosed to clients. Players with Perception rank 7+ notice the log on Vorell's desk during their first visit. What they do with that information is a player choice.

*Most powerful enchantment service in Ironthorn. Permanent options with alignment cost. Every commission logged and reported to the Throne.*

---

### City management office — Office of Tolerance
The city's formal acknowledgement that certain activities are neither licensed nor prosecuted. Issues non-interference certificates — documents that state, in precise bureaucratic language, that the city has decided not to have a position on a specific activity at a specific location. The certificate does not authorise the activity. It documents the city's decision not to stop it.

The Office of Tolerance has been in continuous operation for sixty years. It has survived four city administrations, two Covenant oversight reviews, and one Iron Dominion inspection that concluded, in writing, that the office was "a pragmatic instrument of civic stability."

**Head officer:** Tolerance Director **Sevin** (Tier 3) — nineteen years in post, alignment publicly unreadable (Perception rank 9+ reveals a Gray alignment with Throne-adjacent tendencies). Sevin knows every faction's business in the Wound Market, has non-interference certificates on file for most of it, and has cultivated relationships with every major player in the lower city over two decades. She is the most information-rich NPC in the lower city and shares nothing without receiving something of equal value.

**Quest function:** Every lower-city faction quest that involves something the city technically should not permit routes through Sevin's office at some point. She is a gatekeeper, a power broker, and — for players who invest in her — a source of intelligence that no other NPC in Ironthorn can match. Her personal questline (mid-game, alignment-independent) involves a non-interference certificate she issued nineteen years ago that she has recently begun to regret.

## Alignment interaction
- Tier I–II: vendors polite and uninformative; treated as tourists; prices high
- Tier III–IV: standard commercial access; nothing offered that was not requested
- Tier V: The Collector notices; a Tier 2 NPC makes eye contact and does not look away first
- Tier VI–VII: market fully open; Quiet Room contact initiates without the player entering first; Sevin schedules a meeting

## Hollows access from this district
- The Quiet Room basement: Throne rank 3+ or alignment Tier VI–VII
- One sealed access point behind Vorell's building: Throne rank 4+ only

## Connections
- West: Ashgate Quarter (open; boundary is atmospheric)
- North: Ledger Row (open; Row's southern end becomes quieter near this boundary)
