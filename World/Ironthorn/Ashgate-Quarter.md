# The Ashgate Quarter

## Overview
Old industry district — forges, tanners, bone merchants, a render yard that has been operating at the city's tolerance for sixty years. The Quarter was the first part of Ironthorn's lower city to be built, and it shows: streets narrower, buildings older, drainage worse. The Ashen Veil does not have an official presence here. It has six buildings, two underground connections, and the passive cooperation of residents who have decided that the alternative is worse.

## Atmosphere
The light reaches the rooftops but does not reach the street. The streets exist in a grey, sourceless dimness that is not darkness but is not light either. Long-term residents have stopped noticing. Newcomers notice immediately.

Reference: Duskwood street-level tone — not dramatic horror, but tired persistence. The people here are pragmatic. They have made accommodations.

## Locations

### Inn — The Ashgate Lodging House
No sign. Known by the district's residents by address. Clean enough, cheap, and completely indifferent to who you are or what hours you keep. The proprietor, **Mossa** (Tier 1), is a large, quiet woman who has run the lodging house for fifteen years and whose policy on questions is that she does not ask them and does not answer them. Rooms have heavy doors. The common room closes at midnight and re-opens at dawn; whatever happens between those hours in the rooms above is not her business.

*Preferred rest location for low-alignment and lower-city-affiliated players. No faction surveillance. No alignment check on entry. Storage access available.*

---

### General vendor — Grundt's Corner
A general goods operation at the Quarter's main street corner. **Grundt** (Tier 1) sells food, tools, lamp oil, rope, and a rotating stock of goods with unclear provenance that he describes, uniformly, as "acquired through estate." Prices are the lowest in the city. Quality varies. He asks no questions and extends the same courtesy to his customers.

*Lowest prices in Ironthorn. Some inventory of unclear origin. Alignment-pricing inverted from upper city: Tier V–VII receive Grundt's best stock and best prices. Tier I–II pay slightly more and receive slightly less.*

---

### Repair station — Davan Ord's Annex
**Davan Ord** (Tier 2) runs his primary forge operation in the Quarter's forge complex and maintains a small annex for quick repairs. Will repair anything — equipment from any faction, any origin, any condition — without comment. Has been repairing things in this district for twenty years. Has noticed certain materials being delivered to nearby buildings at irregular intervals and has been carefully not thinking about what they are used for. He is approaching the point where not-thinking is no longer sustainable.

*Faction-neutral repair. No origin questions. Davan's growing awareness is a mid-game NPC development trigger.*

---

### District-exclusive — The Ashgate Forge complex
Ironthorn's only full forge. The forge complex occupies a full block at the Quarter's industrial centre — multiple furnaces, a metalworking floor, a casting room, and a separate quenching area. Run collectively by three forge masters (all Tier 1–2, all independent, none faction-affiliated).

The forge is faction-neutral by long tradition — upper-city factions send metalwork here because there is nowhere else to send it, which gives the forge masters a structural independence that the Quarter's Veil presence does not threaten. The Veil needs the forge as much as the Covenant does.

**What can be commissioned:** Weapons, armour, advanced metalwork, custom components. Output is material-dependent — the player brings the materials, the forge masters provide the skill. Better materials produce better output. Veil-sourced or Throne-sourced materials can be worked here without comment.

**Davan Ord** handles the most complex commissions personally. His skill is the highest in the city. Players who build a relationship with him over multiple commissions unlock the ability to request items that are not on the standard commission list — pieces that require both exceptional skill and exceptional discretion.

*The only full forge in Ironthorn. All factions use it. Faction-neutral. Material quality determines output quality.*

---

### Guard post — Quarter Watch
Two officers. Stone building near the Quarter's northern entrance. Their mandate, as best as anyone has been able to determine, is to prevent trouble from spilling into Ledger Row rather than to police the Quarter itself. They are present but deliberately incurious. When asked about specific buildings or specific activities, they experience a consistent and selective memory failure.

**Watch Lead Brenk** (Tier 1) has been in this post for nine years. He receives a modest monthly supplement from a source he has chosen not to identify. He is not corrupt in a way that troubles him. He is practical.

*Lowest active surveillance in any Ironthorn district. Present at the northern entrance only — the interior of the Quarter is effectively unpatrolled.*

---

### Notice board — The Ashgate Board
A weathered board on the Quarter's main street, handwritten notices, unsigned postings, job offers with no employer named, and a rotating set of coded communications that the Ashen Veil uses for operational messaging. Players with Veil rank 2+ or Perception rank 8+ can identify the Veil's coded postings. The board also carries legitimate notices — work available at the forges, rooms for rent, lost items — mixed in with everything else. The mixture is deliberate.

---

### General trade broker — Cress's Dealing House
**Cress** (Tier 2) — who also owns the render yard — operates a secondary trade brokerage from her main building. Handles goods that cannot be moved through the Ledger Row Exchange without questions being asked. Sources materials from both the Quarter's industry and from Veil-adjacent supply chains she does not describe. Her inventory includes lower-city-exclusive goods unavailable in the upper city and a consignment service that asks only one question: cash or trade?

*Lower-city trade network. Veil-adjacent supply chain. No origin questions. Broader dark-economy inventory than any upper-city broker.*

---

### Alchemist — Pell's Back Room
No storefront. Accessed through the lodging house's kitchen — Mossa points the way for players who ask correctly. **Pell** (Tier 2) is an unlicensed alchemist producing compounds that Mirren in the Sanctum Ward considers impossible and that Verath in the Greenward produces through Circle-reagent workarounds. Pell does it with lower-city materials and fewer scruples. His formulations are effective and occasionally unpredictable. He has been operating in the Quarter for six years without a licence and considers this a sustainable situation.

*Unlicensed alchemy. Lower-city reagents. Broader dark-alignment formulation access. Some compounds carry minor side effects Pell considers within acceptable parameters.*

---

### City management office — Office of Works
Infrastructure maintenance, construction permits, drainage, and — on paper — the Hollows. The Office of Works is the only city office with documented jurisdiction over the sub-city, by virtue of the sealing order being filed here three hundred years ago.

**Head officer:** Works Director **Havel** (Tier 2) — a practical man who inherited a filing cabinet full of records he has not had the time to read fully and a legal responsibility for a sub-city he has no intention of entering. He is aware the sealing record is incomplete. He has decided this is a previous administration's problem.

**The incomplete sealing record:** Filed in a cabinet in the Office of Works. The official record of the Hollows sealing documents the decision and the date but not the cause — the section describing the event that prompted the sealing is missing. Not damaged, not water-stained. Missing. Three pages, cleanly removed. Havel knows they are missing. He has not reported this to any office. Players who access the cabinet (Perception rank 6+ to notice the gap, or a specific quest trigger) receive the first confirmation that the sealing was deliberate concealment rather than administrative response to a structural problem.

**Quest function:** Construction disputes, drainage crises used as cover for Veil-related quests, and the primary paper trail for the Hollows sealing all route through this office.

## Alignment interaction
- Tier I–II: ambient discomfort; NPCs monosyllabic; prices slightly higher; some doors do not open
- Tier III–IV: neutral; treated as outsiders who are not a problem
- Tier V–VII: the Quarter opens — fuller inventory, certain doors left ajar, Dael initiates conversation

## Hollows access from this district
- Ashgate Lane building (southern end): Veil rank 2+ or specific quest
- Render yard grate (eastern edge): Perception rank 8+ to notice; no faction requirement once found
- Dael's clinic passage: unlocks through Dael's personal questline
