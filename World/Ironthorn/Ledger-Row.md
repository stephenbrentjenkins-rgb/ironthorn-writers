# Ledger Row

## Overview
The commercial spine of Ironthorn — banks, brokerages, cartographers, contract houses, and a currency exchange that has been operating continuously for one hundred and forty years under six different ownership names, all of which are Gray Compact fronts. The Row runs north to south through the centre of the city, crossing the light/shadow divide at its midpoint. The Compact did not cause this geography. They selected their location because of it.

The architecture is clean, functional, no faction sigils, well-maintained but not ostentatious. The people who work here are professionally friendly. They will answer your question and process your transaction. They will remember everything you told them.

## Atmosphere
The only district where the light/shadow transition is physically perceptible as you walk through it. At the northern end, the light is the Sanctum Ward's natural quality. By the southern end, something has shifted — not dramatically, but enough to notice. The midpoint is where the Compact's primary building stands. This is not a coincidence they acknowledge.

## Locations

### Inn — The Neutral Table
Technically a tavern with rooms above, but the rooms are the point. Located at the precise geographic centre of the Row — equidistant from both sides of the city. Popular with off-duty garrison soldiers, independent merchants, and anyone who needs to meet someone from the other side of the city without it being noted.

The barkeep, **Orvan Sel** (Tier 2), is faction-unaffiliated and intends to remain so. He is very good at not hearing things. Conversations held at the Neutral Table do not trigger NPC surveillance flags that conversations in faction-controlled spaces do — this is a mechanical guarantee, not a social convention.

*The only neutral meeting location in Ironthorn. All alignment tiers served equally. No faction surveillance. Rooms available above.*

---

### General vendor — The Row Supplier
A mid-size general goods operation halfway along the Row. Stocks a broader range than district vendors typically carry — the Row's central position means it sources from both sides of the city without comment. The vendor (Tier 1, rotates monthly — always a different face, always equally pleasant) sells standard consumables, tools, and a small selection of goods from both the Sanctum Ward and the Ashgate Quarter that would not typically share shelf space.

*Widest general inventory of any district vendor. Slight premium for the convenience. Monthly rotation of vendor staff is a Compact operational security measure.*

---

### Repair station — Hallum's Bench
A repair operation on the Row's eastern side. **Hallum** (Tier 2) is a licensed craftsman who will repair anything brought to him, from either side of the city, without commentary. He is the only repair operator in Ironthorn who will work on blood-magic-enhanced items without requiring Throne standing — he considers himself a craftsman, not a moralist, and charges extra for the professional neutrality. Does not have a full forge; for new fabrication, he refers clients to the Ashgate Quarter.

*Faction-neutral repair. Will service items from any origin. Does not report to any faction office.*

---

### Guard post — The Row Patrol
The Row's guard presence is the most unusual in the city: three officers assigned from the city garrison (not Covenant-directed), rotating in eight-hour shifts, whose primary mandate is commercial dispute resolution rather than security enforcement. They carry weapons but almost never use them. Their actual function is to make the Row feel safe enough for business to continue — which it does.

The current patrol lead, **Officer Maret** (Tier 2), has been in this post for six years. She has an informal arrangement with Factor Renne Saul that neither party has committed to paper: she tells him when someone is asking unusual questions about the Row's buildings, and he ensures her annual performance review is always positive.

*Commercial-dispute focused. Lowest confrontation threshold in the city. Maret's Compact arrangement makes her a passive intelligence asset.*

---

### Notice board — The Row Board
A large double-sided board at the Row's midpoint — one side for the upper city, one side for the lower city, though the distinction has blurred over years of use. Commercial postings, trade opportunities, wanted notices, and a significant volume of coded messages that Compact operatives use for internal communication. Players with Compact rank 2+ or Perception rank 7+ can identify the coded postings. Players with Compact rank 4+ can read them.

---

### General trade broker — The Exchange
The Row's primary trade brokerage and the Compact's most public-facing operation. Processes coin, negotiates letters of credit, handles consignment and long-distance trade arrangements. The teller windows are staffed by Compact junior operatives gaining field experience.

**Factor Renne Saul** (Tier 4) operates from the back office. Players who reach Compact rank 2 meet him here. The Exchange has the widest trade network of any brokerage in Ironthorn — goods from both sides of the city, connections to outside-city trade routes, and a private archive of consignment records that constitutes a partial inventory of what every significant person in Ironthorn owns and owes.

*Widest trade network in the city. Back-office access at Compact rank 2+. Exchange records are the Compact's primary leverage database for Ironthorn.*

---

### Alchemist — The Meridian Compound
A licensed alchemical operation at the Row's midpoint. Produces standard and advanced compounds — more sophisticated than Mirren's in the Sanctum Ward, less botanically specialised than Verath's in the Greenward. The alchemist, **Doss** (Tier 2), sources from both sides of the city and will formulate to order without excessive legal scrupulousness, provided the order is placed through the right channel. The right channel is introduced by Compact rank 2+ standing.

*Standard plus extended inventory. Custom formulation available at Compact rank 2+. Sources reagents from both upper and lower city.*

---

### District-exclusive — Cartography House and scribe services
The Row's cartography operation — Ironthorn's only licensed map-maker and document preparation service.

**Public services:** City maps, regional maps, trade route documentation, legal document preparation, contract drafting. All available without faction standing.

**Back-room services** (Compact rank 2+ or Deception skill 6+):
- Document alteration and false identity papers
- Certified copies of documents that do not officially exist
- Access to the incomplete Hollows map — seventeen years old, three-quarters complete, stopped at a point the cartographer apparently decided not to continue past

**The cartographer on staff** (Tier 2) is a legitimate professional who has worked here for eight years and is aware that the back-room services exist. She considers this a reasonable division of labour and does not ask questions about the documents she is not shown.

*The Hollows map is a key mid-game intelligence item. The final notation — "further passage blocked — not by structure — recommend discontinuation" — is the first textual evidence of the Void Eternum's presence beneath Ironthorn.*

---

### City management office — Office of the Treasury
Handles city tax collection, gate revenue accounting, trade licence fees, and the distribution of wages to city employees — including the Guards of the Ward and the Row Patrol. The Treasury is the office that pays everyone, which means it is the office with the most leverage over everyone, and it is located in Ledger Row, not in the Sanctum Ward.

This is the source of the city's primary institutional tension: the Covenant supervises the Office of the Record, but it cannot control the Office of the Treasury, and the Treasury controls the Covenant's city-funded operations through the wage ledger.

**Head officer:** Treasurer **Vasken Oull** (Tier 3) — competent, Gray-aligned, and completely aware that his office's position is the reason the Compact chose Ledger Row as a base. He has not been bought. He has been made to understand that his interests and the Compact's interests are structurally aligned, which is better.

**Quest function:** City funding disputes, guard loyalty quests (who does a guard actually work for when their wages and their orders come from different factions?), and the budget trail for the Inquisitorial Sub-Order's operations — which are paid from a line item labelled "relic recovery" that Vasken has never been given documentation for.

## Alignment interaction
- All alignment tiers can access and transact in Ledger Row
- Tier I–II: served with visible wariness; principled people are unpredictable
- Tier IV: the Row's preferred client; atmosphere relaxes perceptibly
- Tier VI–VII: served at a premium; passive alert to Factor Saul within the hour
