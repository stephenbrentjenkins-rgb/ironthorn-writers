# The Sanctum Ward

## Overview
The civic and spiritual heart of Ironthorn. The Aureate Covenant's chapter house dominates the northern end of the ward — cathedral architecture, gilded stonework, windows tall enough to let in a column of light even when the sky is grey. The main market square sits at the ward's centre: permanent stalls, seasonal vendors, a public notice board that the Covenant updates weekly with proclamations nobody reads carefully enough. The Hall of Records occupies the eastern edge — city census, property deeds, trade licences, and one sealed sub-basement that is not on the public floor plan.

Everything in the Sanctum Ward is maintained, visible, and watched. The cobblestones are even. The gutters are clear. Guards of the Ward walk the main routes in pairs at all hours. They are polite. They remember faces.

## Atmosphere
The light here is natural and plentiful. It arrives at the right angle in the morning, sits at the right height at noon, and leaves with the right colour at dusk. The Sanctum Ward is the part of Ironthorn where the city still believes its own story about itself.

Reference: Elwynn Forest cathedral-light quality. Goldshire market square energy — busy, social, fundamentally reassuring. The reassurance is real. It is also incomplete.

## Locations

### Inn — The Hearthstone
Ironthorn's largest and most socially central inn. Common room runs from breakfast through midnight. Private rooms above. Clean, well-lit, and priced accordingly. The barkeep, **Solta Byrne** (Tier 2), has run the Hearthstone for twenty-two years and has heard most of what there is to hear in the Ward. She does not share it unprompted, but she does not forget it either. Players who build rapport with Solta over multiple visits unlock ambient dialogue that functions as a district-level intelligence feed.

*Storage access available. Safe conversation location — no faction surveillance flags trigger here.*

---

### General vendor — Arnath's Provisions
Ground-floor shop on the market square's east side. Sells food, basic tools, rope, lamp oil, and common consumables. Quality is good; prices are upper-city. Arnath himself (Tier 1) is unremarkable. His assistant, a young woman who is almost certainly a Compact junior field operative, is slightly too attentive to customer conversations.

*Alignment pricing: Tier I–II receive a modest discount. Tier VI–VII are served without comment but charged above the listed price.*

---

### Repair station — Settled & Sons
A licensed craftsman's workshop one block west of the market square. Equipment repair, basic tool fabrication, and minor weapon maintenance. Guild-registered. Will not repair items with obviously illegal provenance without a convincing explanation. The owner's son, who does most of the actual work, is less particular than his father and handles the after-hours counter.

*Upper-city repair quality. Cannot repair blood-magic-enhanced or Veil-sourced equipment — those require the Ashgate forge.*

---

### Guard post — Ward Station
The headquarters of the Guards of the Ward — the Covenant-directed, city-paid patrol unit for the Sanctum Ward. Shift changes visible from the market square. The current Watch Commander, **Ser Orvane** (Tier 2), is a twenty-year veteran who privately disagrees with the Covenant on several points and has not said so to anyone. He runs an orderly post, responds to reports promptly, and maintains a register of persons of interest that the Covenant's Inquisitorial Sub-Order accesses without asking.

*Alignment checkpoint: Tier V+ players are passively flagged after extended Ward presence. Not confronted — observed. Tier VII players are reported to Inquisitor-Commander Leth within the hour.*

---

### Notice board — The Ward Proclamation Board
Stone-mounted board beside the market square fountain. Official Covenant proclamations, approved missing-person notices, licensed trade announcements. Managed postings only — a Ward officer reviews new additions every morning. The board is a reliable source of early-game quest hooks and faction-reputation-building opportunities, but nothing posted here is unvetted. The most interesting information in the Ward travels by word of mouth, not board posting.

---

### General trade broker — The Square Broker
A licensed trade intermediary operating from a permanent booth in the market square. Buys and sells goods across district lines, handles consignment for vendors who cannot physically operate in the Ward. The Square Broker has connections to Ledger Row's Exchange and will, for a fee, source materials from other districts without the player needing to visit them. Does not deal in goods of unclear provenance — or rather, does not acknowledge doing so.

---

### Alchemist — Mirren's Compound
A small, precise shop on the Ward's northern edge, close to the Chapter House. Licensed reagents, standard healing compounds, antitoxins, and basic crafting components. Mirren (Tier 2) is fastidiously legal and will not formulate anything the Covenant has not approved. She keeps meticulous records and submits them to the Chapter House quarterly. Players who build trust with Mirren unlock access to a small set of advanced legal compounds not available in her public inventory.

*Upper-city alchemy only. For unlicensed reagents, players are directed — by the absence of options here — toward the lower city.*

---

### City management office — Office of the Record
The Sanctum Ward's civic office handles city census, property registration, identity documents, and trade licensing. Covenant-supervised but technically city-funded — a distinction that creates ongoing quiet friction with the Treasury office in Ledger Row.

**Head officer:** Registrar Celn Avour (Tier 2) — meticulous, Covenant-loyal, and carrying a key to the sub-basement he has never personally used.

**Sealed sub-basement:** Contains the city's founding records and documents related to the origin of the light/shadow divide. Access requires Covenant rank 5+ OR a specific quest trigger through Maren Voss. The sub-basement also connects to the Hollows — this connection is not in any document the Registrar has been shown.

**Quest function:** Property disputes, identity verification quests, and the main paper trail for the Covenant's Inquisitorial Sub-Order's operations all route through this office.

---

### Faction-specific locations

**The Covenant Chapter House**
Public front: shrine hall, alms station, weekly services schedule. Inner access (rank 2+): administrative offices, Inquisitorial Sub-Order operations, Archivist Maren Voss's reading room. Deep access (rank 5+): the sub-basement connection. The Chapter House is the Ward's most information-dense location and the one most likely to reward sustained Covenant investment — and to punish betrayal most severely.

**The Healer's Annex**
A small Covenant-operated clinic adjacent to the Chapter House. Free healing for Ward residents; reduced-cost healing for visitors. The cleric on duty rotates weekly. One of the rotating clerics is not Covenant-affiliated — she is a Verdant Circle observer placed here under an informal inter-faction arrangement that neither faction has committed to writing.

## Alignment interaction
- Tier I–III: fully welcomed; guards nod; Covenant NPCs initiate conversation; vendor discounts
- Tier IV: served without warmth; passive tail begins after extended presence (non-hostile)
- Tier V: guard presence increases; Covenant NPCs become formal and brief
- Tier VI–VII: Inquisitorial alert sent; player not confronted immediately — something begins
